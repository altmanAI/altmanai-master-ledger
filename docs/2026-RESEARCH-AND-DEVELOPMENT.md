## Purpose

AltmanAI's 2026 research and development program exists to turn human-first artificial intelligence principles into practical, testable systems. The program prioritizes useful software, accountable human authorization, inspectable evidence, honest capability boundaries, and measurable Proof-of-Impact.

The operating sequence is:

> **Human authorization → AltmanAI Model 2.0 execution → Proof-of-Impact validation**

AltmanAI Model 2.0 may assist with research, architecture, code, testing, documentation, analysis, and repository execution within an authorized scope. Final corporate authority and accountability remain human.

## Research rules

1. **Human authority is explicit.** Material actions require authorization appropriate to their risk.
2. **Status must be accurate.** Proposed, experimental, in development, working, released, and verified are not interchangeable.
3. **Evidence accompanies important claims.** Tests, hashes, receipts, sources, decision records, or reproducible artifacts should support material statements.
4. **AI assistance is disclosed.** AltmanAI-controlled records attribute material AI work to **AltmanAI Model 2.0**.
5. **Security and privacy are product requirements.** Secrets, private data, and sensitive implementation details do not belong in public records.
6. **Historical integrity is preserved.** Signed, sealed, minted, or hash-bound records are corrected through amendments or versioned successors rather than silent rewriting.
7. **Human impact is reviewed.** Systems should strengthen human agency, clarity, accessibility, dignity, and control.

## 2026 execution priorities

- Formalize the P.A.I.H.I. specification and consolidate its working scorer and integration guidance.
- Advance DailyPilot from a tested reference engine toward reliable product integration.
- Harden and package the AltmanAI SDK with stronger tests, documentation, release controls, and integration examples.
- Build ReleaseShield's narrow, evidence-producing CLI foundation before expanding its claims.
- Add repository-level `PROOF.md`, decision logs, dependency records, security checks, and measurable success criteria across strategic repositories.
- Improve public portfolio routing so developers, partners, users, and reviewers can distinguish active systems from historical or experimental work.
- Build credible Pittsburgh technology impact through useful products, documented execution, and evidence-backed relationships.

## Measurement

The program will be evaluated through:

- shipped and testable artifacts;
- documented human approvals;
- reproducible checks and test results;
- corrected or retired unsupported claims;
- security, privacy, accessibility, and dependency evidence;
- adoption and user-value metrics when those measurements become available; and
- public Proof-of-Impact records that distinguish evidence from aspiration.
