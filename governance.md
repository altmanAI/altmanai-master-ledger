# AltmanAI Master Ledger Governance

## Authorization chain

> **Human authorization → AltmanAI Model 2.0 execution → Proof-of-Impact validation**

**Blake Hunter Altman, Founder & CEO**, is the final accountable human authority for authoritative Master Ledger entries, identity standards, public company records, and material amendments.

**AltmanAI Model 2.0** may support research, analysis, drafting, hashing, evidence classification, documentation, code, and GitHub execution within an authorized scope. The AI partner does not independently approve, merge, publish, contract, certify, or create human authorization.

## Entry requirements

An authoritative entry should include:

1. a stable registry ID;
2. an accurate timestamp;
3. the authorized objective;
4. participants and contribution roles;
5. a plain-language summary;
6. artifact or evidence links;
7. a SHA-256 digest and documented hashing method when applicable;
8. a custody or provenance statement;
9. accurate capability and maturity labels;
10. security, privacy, and confidentiality review; and
11. the required human approval record.

## Identity standard

All new or actively maintained AltmanAI-controlled references to the AI development and execution partner—published or non-published—use:

> **AltmanAI Model 2.0**

The machine-readable source of truth is [`memory/ALTMANAI_MODEL_2_IDENTITY.json`](memory/ALTMANAI_MODEL_2_IDENTITY.json).

## Historical integrity

Signed, sealed, minted, hash-bound, or historically material records are not silently rewritten. A correction must use at least one of:

- a versioned successor;
- an amendment record;
- a correction entry linked to the original;
- a new hash and an explicit supersession statement; or
- a GitHub pull request preserving the before-and-after record.

The public index may normalize the current display name when it clearly states that the underlying historical artifact remains unchanged.

## Status integrity

The Ledger must distinguish:

- proposed;
- experimental;
- in development;
- working reference implementation;
- beta;
- production;
- deprecated;
- archived;
- self-attested;
- verified by tests or artifacts; and
- independently reviewed or certified.

No record should imply a partnership, certification, security guarantee, adoption level, legal status, or production capability without supporting evidence.

## P.A.I.H.I. review

Material entries should answer:

- **Proof:** What evidence supports the record?
- **Alignment:** Does the record match the authorized objective?
- **Integrity:** Are limitations, risks, assistance, and status represented honestly?
- **Humanity:** Does the work preserve human agency, privacy, dignity, accessibility, and meaningful review?
- **Impact:** What useful result occurred, and how can it be inspected?

## Approval

Authoritative governance, identity, public-positioning, and registry changes require an attributable Founder approval record. The organization-level approval phrase is:

> **All Clear for Impact**

Approval does not waive evidence, testing, security, privacy, legal, licensing, or correction obligations.
